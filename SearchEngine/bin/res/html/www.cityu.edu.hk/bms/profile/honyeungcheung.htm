Dr Hon-Yeung Cheung received his Bachelor degree in Pharmacy and became a qualified pharmacist in 1974. After three years of practice in pharmaceutical manufacturing sector, he pursued a postgraduate research degree in Pharmaceutical Microbiology and was awarded a PhD degree in 1981. At the end of the same year, he was awarded a Fogarty Research Fellowship from the United States Government for three years and worked as a postdoctoral trainee in molecular biology at National Institutes of Health (1981–83). He commenced his research in oncogene at the Uniformed Service University of Health Sciences (1984–85). The results of this work were subsequently published in Science. In 1986 he accepted an offer from Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Organization in Australia as a Senior Research Scientist to develop recombinant subunit vaccine. He returned to Hong Kong to commence his academic career at CityU in 1989 up to present. His research interests and specialism include molecular differentiation of microbial cells, analysis and mechanistic studies of pharmaceutical products, traditional Chinese medicines, biological products, and environmental toxicants. He has published more than 180 papers and over 100 commentaries in different media. Throughout the last two decades, he has served as committee member, adviser and scientific member in different public organizations. Currently, he is the Editor-in-Chief of Hong Kong Pharmaceutical Journal, Managing Director of a technology centre and a key member of the Research Group for Bioactive Products.
