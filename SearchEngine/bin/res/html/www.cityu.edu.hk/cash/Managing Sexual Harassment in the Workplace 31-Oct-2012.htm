Managing Sexual Harassment in the Workplace

處理工作間性騷擾

 

Intended Participants

:

Heads of Departments/Academic/Teaching/

Administrative & Executive Staff

Class Size

:

60 pax

Duration

:

3 hours

Date

:

Class 1:    31 October 2012    (English)

Class 2:    24 January 2013     (Cantonese)

Time

:

3:00 p.m. - 6:00 p.m.

Venue

:

To be confirmed

Language of Instruction

:

Class 1: English

Class 2: Cantonese

Instructor

:

Ms Cynthia Lam, Senior Training Officer from Equal Opportunities Commission

Objective

·         To define sexual harassment and identify behavior which constitutes sexual harassment.

·         To identify the impact of sexual harassment in the workplace.

·         To understand the liability for sexual harassment.

·         To explore measures that organizations can take to prevent sexual harassment.

·         To explore effective management responses to sexual harassment.

 

Content

1.      Understanding sexual harassment

a.          Who are protected under the law – students, staff members, customers

b.          Types of sexual harassment

i.            Person to person harassment

ii.          Sexually hostile environment

2.      Sexual harassment in context

a.          What is meant by unwelcome?

b.          Relevant circumstances

c.          Relevance of intention

3.      Legal Liabilities and Defence

a.          Civil vs criminal

b.          Legal liabilities: individuals, accessory and employers’ vicarious liability

c.          Defence – Why should organizations deal with sexual harassment complaints and what are the reasonably practicable steps to take

4.      Impact/cost of Sexual Harassment

a.          On the employees

b.          On the organization

5.      University policy on sexual harassment

6.      Preventing and responding effectively to sexual harassment complaints

a.          Roles and responsibilities of supervisors

b.          Understanding the parties (harassers and victims)

c.          Options for complaint resolution – helping aggrieved party to make an informed decision

d.          Principles in handling sexual harassment complaints

 

Enquiries

Vickie LAM (Telephone : 3442 9082)

 

Remarks

The course is subject to cancellation due to under-subscription.
