Investigation Skills for Sexual Harassment

 

Target Participants

:

Staff members who may deal with complaints of discrimination and harassment

Class Size

:

25 pax

Date

:

18 May 2012 (Friday)

Time

:

9:00 a.m. - 6:00 p.m. (Lunch break : 12:30 pm – 2:30 pm)

Venue

:

Seminar Room 3, SCOPE

Language of Instruction

:

English

Instructor

:

Ms Cynthia Lam, Senior Training Officer from Equal Opportunities Commission (EOC)

Objectives

1.      To identify the options for resolving workplace complaints

2.      To explore how staff and students can be supported to resolve complaints at the earliest possible instance

3.      To identify effective intervention strategies to contain inappropriate behaviour

4.      To identify effective ways to resolve complaints on an informal basis

5.      To explore issues involved in investigation of formal complaints

Content

1.             Overview of complaint handling

(a)           Impact / cost of discrimination / harassment

(b)          Understanding why people don’t complain

(c)           Helping staff and students make an informed decision

(d)          Principles in complaint handling: prompt handling, victim-friendly approach, giving assistance and support

2.             Resolving workplace complaint - Self Management Approach

(a)           Elements, advantages and disadvantages of self-management approach

(b)          Supporting staff and students to resolve complaints at the earliest possible instance

3.             Resolving workplace complaint - Informal Resolution

(a)           Elements, advantages and disadvantages of informal resolution

(b)          Preventive measures

(c)           Effective early intervention

(d)          Mediating disputes

4.             Resolving workplace complaint - Formal Investigative Processes

(a)           When to use formal investigative processes

(b)          Elements, advantages and disadvantages of formal investigative processes

(c)           Issues in investigation: timeframes, documentation, confidentiality, conflict of interest, procedural fairness, substantive fairness, standards of proof, decision-making and recommendations, mitigating factors, outcome and related issues

5.             Practical sessions – Participants will take part in role-play where they could practise their skills in handling staff complaints

Approach

This workshop is divided into two sessions – Morning session on the lecture and afternoon session on role-play.

Enquiries

Wendy YUEN (Telephone : 3442 9432)

Remark

The course is subject to cancellation due to under-subscription
