    Ho Long Yi, Christy Loughborough University, United Kingdom
    Feb 15
    Hurray! I am finally inside my cozy room in Loughborough Harry French Court after travelling for almost 21 hours! Not what I expected but my room became a huge mess within 2 days. This is the truth of living in a self-catered hall where no one would do the housework for you. #Sad_But_True☹
    Here is the pantry where my five hallmates and I cook and have gathering here. I used to think that it would be difficult to join into local students’ social life. However, I am surprised and overwhelmed by the kindness and hospitality of the people here. Susan secretly put some cookies on my desk to cheer me up from homesick. Max and Connon taught me that Loughborough does not pronounce as ‘law-bo-roar’, instead, it pronounces as ‘love-brar’. I love this name very much, because this place is really full of ‘love’ <3 #cookie_is_the_best_cure
    Feb 28
    My exchange life has started for 3 weeks already. My favorite place must be Rutland Building, which is the first teaching campus of Loughborough University in 1938. It turned into a student enquiry center with trophies won by students and different sports teams displayed along the long aisle. #Strong_sense_of_belonging
    If I see any ‘ReVIEW’ sign inside the lecture hall, it means the lesson will be a lot ‘EASIER’! It is a lecture capture system which would record the teaching session and available for students to view on LEARN platform. As students could review lecturer’s teaching at home afterward, it would be an excuse for me to, occasionally, be in a daze!
    With the great help of ‘ReVIEW’, students will not be busy copying notes. Instead, they would prepare few blank papers to write down any pop-up idea and even out-of-syllabus notes (shown on the right screen). It shows that they are very willing to learn.
    Mar 15
    Loughborough University well-deserves its reputation as the “University of the Year for Sport” (The Times, 2014). Can you believe that there are 43 sports stadiums inside the campus and each of them meets the national standard? Even Loughborough University FC has its own football stadium! #A_passerby_may_be_the_next_Beckham
    Undoubtedly, exchange life could easily turn a thin person into a fat one. No-one else would care about a balanced diet when the prices of steak and pork are so low. As a result, I joined the My Lifestyle Programme, a programme designed for students who want to exercise, keep fit, and play sport. This was the first time I tried fencing. I met two French people and one Italian during the lesson who were also amateurs. The four of us enjoyed fencing so much, we felt as though we were Harry Potter and Malfoy duelling! #Die_hard_Harry_Potter_fans
    Mar 20
    It is not usual to see such a beautiful blue sky in United Kingdom’s City of Fog. Due to the adverse weather the previous week, many trains and flights were either delayed or cancelled. Except in Loughborough, which is in the middle of England, where the weather was so much better than in other cities. As such, I took advantage of the lovely weather and went to Loughborough market, which is sort of like a small-scale flea market. You could buy hot food, drinks, dry goods, flowers, and even hand-made soap. #Housewife_lifestyle
    The sock statue is the most famous symbol in Loughborough town centre. The man is seated on a pillar, wearing only a leaf for underwear and a sock – which he is looking down at admiringly. It is located in front of the town hall and local people love to touch it as they pass by. #Loughborough_lucky_symbol
    Apr 1
    Going on a local tour is one of the best ways to explore the world and better understand the local people. The Kelpies is a 30-metre high cutlery-made horse head sculpture. Athena, my Taiwanese hall mate, Christy Choi and I would not have discovered this amazing statue without our local guide. #Thanks_Rabbie’s_Tour_for_this_wonderful_trip
    Highland cattle, with their unkempt hair and foolish way of eating, are such cutie pies. They are a unique Scottish breed which is the oldest history in the world. However, they are really hard to find across the vast expanses of grassland. How lucky I was to meet them in person! #Top_things_to_see_in_Scotland
    Sean must be one of the best tour guides I have ever met. Before retiring, he was a history teacher, and now, instead of teaching students about Scotland’s history, he teaches tourists. He knew almost every story behind each historical building. Most of all, I liked his cheerful nature, which made my Western Highland tour full of laughter. #Shy_to_ask_for_the_photo
    Apr 15
    The University of Cambridge is the second-oldest university in UK and is more than 800 years old. It is famous for its stunning buildings and its 11 colleges. My favourite definitely being the Bridge of Sighs. I can relate very strongly with the story behind the name. When exams were near, students would go there to sigh. The echoes could be heard all along the bridge, giving it its sad atmosphere. Students around the world are all the same – we are all afraid of midterms and exams! #I_sigh_for_my_assignments_and projects_as_well
    Besides walking around the colleges, punting is one of the best way to discover the beauty of Cambridge. ‘Saying Goodbye to Cambridge Again’ by Xu zhi-mo describes “just to pole a boat upstream to seek a dream”. Me and a friend sought our dream, which was to enjoy the views on both sides of the river and chit-chat with the local boatman. #even_the_boatman_knows_the_meaning_of_the_poem #the_power_of_tourists
    Apr 21
    I really believe in the motto, “Travelling can help gather the world together”. Han Yeeun from Hertfordshire, UK, Yuki from Lund, Sweden, and I from Loughborough, UK, met in Oxford to follow in the footsteps of Alice in Wonderland. We had a great brunch in the covered market, where fifty quality independent shops are located. #Girls_Travelling_=_Girls_Talk
    Speaking of Oxford, I bet 90% of you think this is the dining hall at Hogwarts in Harry Potter. In fact, it is the dining hall of Christ Church College, Oxford University. While there is no Professor Dumbledore announcing the start of dinner, or a headless knight jumping out and scaring the students, the whole place really reminds me of the movie. Yuki said she wanted to be a student at Oxford so that she could enjoy the atmosphere at every meal. However, I disagree. I feel that the best sights are those you remember rather than the ones you see every day. #Harry_Potter_is_the_best_tourist_attraction

    May 1
    It is not easy to find a music room in a university which is strongly focused on student sports development. It is even harder to find a student who knows where the music practice rooms can be found. LU ARTS is located off-campus, where a number of music rooms are available for ‘Music VIP’ members to use free of charge. #I_guess_only_1%_of_LU_students_holds_a_Music_’VIP’_and_I_am_one_of_them
    Being an exchange student for three months and saying “I don’t miss home” would be a lie. However, life must go on and music is my way to release stress. Music can heal one’s heart; and while I’m out of my comfort zone, I can definitely feel the power of music. Playing piano, which I have done for fifteen years, helps me remember the happiest moments of my life in Hong Kong. When playing piano, all I have to do is look out of the window and see the clear sky and I know that I am not alone. Somebody is waiting for me in Hong Kong – a more mature Christy. #I_stayed_in_the_piano_room_for_over_5_hours #Didn’t_even_ working_this_hard_for_my_piano_examination
