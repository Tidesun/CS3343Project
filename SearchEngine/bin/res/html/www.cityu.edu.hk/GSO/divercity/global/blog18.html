CityU Omnibus on the Silk Road: A Summer in Prague

Text: Richard Finnigan,
Media & Communication

Photos: Charles University and 7 programme participants

To feel in one’s presence, and see with one’s eyes is the key to personal enrichment. This holds particularly true when travelling to a completely new part of the world. From June 21st to July 22nd of 2017, seven City University students did just this by joining the CityU Omnibus on the Silk Road: A Summer in Prague. For an entire 4 weeks, the culture and lifestyle of the Czech Republic was at the fingertips of our students. In this relatively short amount of time, memories were created that will last a lifetime and an appreciation was fostered for an entirely new way of living. As one of the participants Sophie Law recalls: “The CityU Omnibus programme is inspiring through local immersion and meeting new people from different backgrounds”.

One such excursion was to the awe-inspiring UNESCO town of Kutna Hora. An experience that both thrilled and chilled our students to the bone, quite literally! The Sedlec Ossuary, a small Roman Catholic chapel, is unlike any other chapel in the world. Furnished with beautiful works created from real human bone taken from victims of the black plague, the Church draws visitors from all corners of the globe in showing breath-taking pieces of craftsmanship. Leaving a disturbingly fascinating impact upon our students, it was a trip that will certainly never be forgotten – evidently that upon asking where the students visited, Kutna Hora was their collective first response.
While being thrust into Czech history through captivating works within Kutna Hora, our students also had the chance to ease their senses in one of Europe’s most picturesque towns – Cesky Krumlov. A Prague in miniature form, the town is dominated by a 13th Century castle and museum containing Gothic, Renaissance and baroque elements. Students were given the opportunity to peruse the quaint streets and absorb the history surrounding them. Prague itself offered immeasurable amounts of cultural experiences, from Classical concerts including Swan Lake, to the Black Light theatre, a show focusing on audience involvement and light exhibitions. Our students were introduced to the vast contrast of historical culture and modern entertainment that Prague offers.

    

City University’s Omnibus programme not only aims to help students to enjoy a productive and memorable summer within Prague, we also feel it is extremely important for the mind to be opened and for horizons to be broadened. Experiencing cultural differences such as tipping culture, foreign cuisines, beer culture and street performances gave our students first-hand experience about an entirely new way of living. Lectures detailing the historical background of German Jewish culture helped students to delve into the very fabric of Czech history while studying the works of the literary great Franz Kafka helped gain a new perspective on Czech writing. Asuka Murayama feels “The Courses were extremely interesting and we had the opportunity to explore Prague to our fullest throughout the programme.”

While asking the students what advice they would give to future participants of the CityU Omnibus programme, they enthusiastically responded that immersing yourself in Czech history and googling modern ways of life in Czech before you arrive probably would help you relate entirely to the significance of the experiences to come and fully enrich your mind with new and exciting encounters with both new people and new sceneries.
