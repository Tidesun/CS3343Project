CityU Omnibus on the Silk Road: Serving Ethnic Minorities

Text: Richard Finnigan,
Media & Communication

Photos: Programme participants

As we immerse ourselves into our modern-day lives, filled with technology, services at the press of a button and towering skyscrapers in every direction, we often lose touch with the simpler things. Those places considered to be “off the beaten path”, that when given the opportunity, present us with an entirely new perspective. CityU Omnibus on the Silk Road – Serving Ethnic Minorities provided irreplaceable experiences for CityU students in the Summer of 2017. The trip to mainland China aims to provide a unique insight into the local customs while aiding the residing children with unforgettable memories and support. As one of the participants Eunice Li, recalls: “Participating in the activity opened my eyes to the diversity of people in mainland China as well as their culture, food and religious belief.”

    

While primarily a serving trip for ethnic minorities, this Omnibus trip offers boundless opportunities for personal growth, team spirit and a chance to experience tranquil landscapes unseen to most eyes. Students were given the opportunity to meet and get to know the ethnic minorities that live in otherwise inaccessible areas to outside visitors. Aksai Kazakh Autonomous Country provided a peaceful, clean and pure experience for the participants as they broke the ice with the native children to feel the warm welcome from the local people. One of participants Lee Wing Yee recalls “We learnt a lot about the situation and the barriers they are facing as a part of ethnic minorities living in a highly urbanized city. For example, the Kazakhs was an ethnic group that used to graze on the grassland. They ride horses as main transportation. Even though nowadays they live in an urbanized city like Aksai, they still sent their children to the mountain beside the city to learn riding houses.”

    

Our students were not introduced blindly to the ethnic minorities. Important classes providing crucial background information detailing their homes, culture, food and what to expect were offered by local governments and families. Meanwhile, the locals also gained a first-hand understanding of Hong Kong culture from our students. As LI Kwan Ni reflects “Interacting with local people was the best way to get the first-hand information of their lives and thoughts and I am glad to introduce Hong Kong to them”. A cultural exchange of ideas and experiences enlightened everyone involved and brought two communities, normally so far apart, closer together.

    

Visiting cultural attractions were also on the agenda for the Omnibus participants. Students were presented with incredible feats of human engineering first hand while visiting Mogao caves, which form a system of 492 temples 25 km southeast of the centre of Dunhuang, an oasis strategically located at a religious and cultural crossroads on the Silk Road recently designated as a UNESCO world heritage site. Furthermore, known as an underground gallery in China, the 13-square-kilometer Fresco Tombs of the Wei and the Jin dynasties impressed students deeply with stunning craftsmanship. Frescos unearthed from the Fresco Tombs, which are also referred to as "an encyclopaedia of the Silk Road" vividly show several of the ancient people's activities along the Silk Road. Not only was the route vital for the transfer of goods, it also acted as a medium for knowledge, technology and religion throughout China.

While asking the participants what advice they would give to future participants, the students emphasised the importance of having an open mind whilst on the excursion. Be open-minded to team members, be open-minded to students from mainland partner universities, be open-minded to ethnic minorities. The bonds they created with the local children will last a lifetime.

    

With such a vast array of opportunities in such a rarely travelled area, student participants feel that it is imperative to gain as much from the experience as possible. Serving the ethnic minorities, embracing a pleasant way of life away from the fast-paced daily routines of Hong Kong and gracing your eyes with incredible works of art from past centuries are just the tip of the iceberg, take this opportunity while you can!
