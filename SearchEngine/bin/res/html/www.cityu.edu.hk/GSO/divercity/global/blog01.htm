11 December 2013

To be a student, but not a free-rider!

Text and photos: Ka-shuen Wai,
Integrated Strategic Communication

When you think of joining the exchange programme, the first thing that pops up in your mind may be “Yeah, we’re gonna throw the notes away and just have fun!” True, going on exchange is something like an once-in-a-lifetime opportunity where you can study abroad for a couple months in a foreign city, and yet travel to every corner of the city at the same time. So I chose to spend my last semester in Seoul, Korea as a student free from pressure. I had been dreaming of shopping crazily in Myeongdong, eating a lot in Busan and going to the beautiful beaches on Jeju Island. Now I was so lucky to really do all these things during my exchange semester in Seoul. What I gained was definitely more than I had expected.

Beautiful Campus
The university where I did my exchange study is called Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU). It has a long history of more than 600 years, and this is why the university has a mixture of antique and modern buildings. I found it amazing to have all these Chinese-style buildings around the school, which make it quite different from the CityU campus. At first I didn’t pay much attention to the stunning temples, even though I passed them every day. Not until the spring came, when all the flowers bloomed, did I realise how important the physical environment is to influence the study atmosphere.

A place to really enjoy
Imagine that you are holding some books, walking slowly down to the foot of a hill with a cool wind blowing and the blossoms of oriental cherry trees fluttering as if it is snowing. Taking in the sight, you breathe in the aroma of the spring air. This is what I did after school. At that moment I realised how lucky I was to be there, and I was determined to dive into my studies at SKKU wholeheartedly. In Hong Kong, I used to play a lot of roles, such as a part-time worker, a private tutor, daughter, student, blogger and girlfriend. I was too busy to enjoy my school life. SKKU, however, was my “secret garden”, where I felt so happy to study and be a student.

Helpful Koreans
Koreans are very hard working and hold a strong sense of responsibility. I was lucky to meet my buddy, Jisu, who was always aiding me in school work, and now we are good friends. As I took several majors in school, I met quite a number of Korean students during class. They were excited to have me there, as I am from Hong Kong, a place that many Koreans would like to visit. They were so kind that they tolerated my poor Korean. At the same time, they appreciated my fluency in English and let me engage in some important roles in student projects. What was more encouraging was that the school administration was really considerate. When we were preparing for mid-term exams, the school distributed “happy bags” to the students, with snacks and magazines inside to encourage us to work hard!

Learning through teamwork
As my major is Integrated Strategic Communication, video productions are an integral part of my studies. At SKKU, I found out for the first time that video making could be fun! We were asked to tell a story about how cultural differences influence interpersonal relationships. I was asked to act as a Korean girl who came back from America and fell in love with a local Korean. It was fun, as I was asked to read out the script entirely in Korean! This project became a remarkable memory from my life in SKKU.

Next time, when someone says “being an exchange student means skipping classes and travelling”, I would say “why not try to engage yourself as a foreign student on a foreign campus and enjoy the school life?” Well, I did not expect to gain so much, but the exchange opportunity has given me the best experience for being a 100% student!
