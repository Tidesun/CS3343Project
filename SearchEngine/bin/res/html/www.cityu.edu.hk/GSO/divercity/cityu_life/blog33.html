A Peek into CityU: Hustle and Bustle of a Campus

Text and photos: Andy Yang,
Business Economics

Located right at the center of the city, CityU’s campus is easily accessible and here, there are events and activities happening every day, academic or extra-curricular. To me, sometimes the hustle and bustle at CityU is overwhelming. With more than ten thousand undergraduate students, high efficiency in service is required.

  
Peak hours at student canteens. Thanks to the efficient workers and servers, we are able to get our food served in a short period of time even when there are a lot of people.

Campus life in CityU is quite vivid and interesting if you make effort in engaging yourself in various activities. Different university units and student societies organize events and activities year round. From sports to music, it’s really up to students to choose what suits them best.


Students rehearsing a musical for the annual CityU Arts Festival.

To help international and non-local students better adjust themselves to study and life in Hong Kong, Global Services Office facilitates meetings between students with different officials to help students get first-hand advices by leaders and authorities.

  
Non-local students at one of the “Meeting with the President” events in September 2017

  
  
Meeting with consulates general for students from particular countries.

Every September, the university holds an Information Day (also known as “Info Day”) for high school students and their family to visit the campus and get to know the different aspects of the university in person. It’s one of the most crowded days on campus. Students from different schools and departments would join and promote their own majors.

  
CityU students and visitors at Information Day, the event to help high school students know the university and its offerings.

Residence life is also an important part of university education. For those who live in student residence, there are different hall activities, and the PEK Cup is one of the annual events to look forward to.

  
Each hall would form their own team for PEK Cup and these teams would compete in various sports like football, badminton, swimming and basketball.

As one of the efforts to promote campus internationalization and integration, subsidy under Student-initiated Projects enables students to gain support from the university to organize events and activities for all students that could serve the purpose to promote a more diverse but welcoming campus at CityU. Global Festival Day organized by International Society is one of the many examples, which brings global cultures to the campus.

  

Global Festival Day booths were setup to represent various countries and showcase different cultures by bringing food, traditional ethnic clothing and art.
