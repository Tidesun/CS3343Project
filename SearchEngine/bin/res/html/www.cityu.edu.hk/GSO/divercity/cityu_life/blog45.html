25 May 2018

Student Initiated Projects: Bringing the World Together at CityU

Text: Irene,
Industrial Engineering and Engineering Management

Photos: Andy Yang,
Business Economics

Design: Ka-man Chan,
Creative Media

How much do you know about the world?

When you walk around the campus, you’ll notice quite many foreign faces here and there. Thanks to CityU’s internationalisation strategies which have brought, literally, the whole world to campus!

Focusing on promoting and celebrating student integration and internationalisation of the campus, Student Initiated Projects (SIP) is one of the many funding schemes enabling students to turn their ideas into action. In groups, students come up with creative ways to promote different cultures through events and activities; they also learnt from the process on how to solicit and manage funding provided by the university. While SIP event organisers gain practical skills through project and event management, their peer students get first-hand information, face-to-face, about other cultures beyond Internet and textbooks.

Being curious about world cultures, I have joined most of SIP events held in the year.

Japanese Day – 2 February 2018

In Hong Kong, Japan, the Land of Rising Sun is hardly an unknown country because she is one of the most popular holiday destinations, say for cherry blossoms and Japanese cuisine! Some of my friends can even say a few words in Japanese!

However, this SIP has brought to the campus something more than just beautiful cherry blossom trees and Japanese food.

Before visiting the Japanese Day, I thought I know Japan pretty well as a student taking a few Japanese culture courses at the uni. However, learning something from the textbook or the web is different from experiencing it in person. At the event, I had the chance to try on a Yukata with the assistance of our fellow students from Japan. Through the outfitting process, I realised that wearing Kimono or Yukata is not just about putting on an outfit but more like performing a traditional ritual.

“Through SIP, we get to work with Japanese exchange students in organising an event which can give CityU students a sense of what a Japanese festival is like.” said Haley Fong and Phoebe Lam, organisers of the Japanese Day.

Unsurprisingly, there were challenges and minor issues when organising the Japanese Day. Our student organisers concluded that overcoming all those difficulties through negotiations and teamwork made the experience so much more valuable.

Be it the learning curve gained from organizing large-scale events such as Japanese Day or an effort to break down the barriers between locals and non-locals, both Haley and Phoebe agreed that SIP events provide a good platform for students to make friends outside of their own comfort circle!

Korean Night – 26 March 2018

“It’s the first ever Korean Night!”

It’s no secret that Hong Kong has a large number of Korean culture fans – either of K-pop or the addictive Korean dramas.

As the first student event on campus to celebrate Korean cultures, our Korean fellows did not disappoint us by putting on a spectacular show, working the crowd into a frenzy of the powerful K-pop dancing and singing. Whether it was a solo or group performance, the audience was in awe throughout the whole night. A variety of all-time favourite Korean food was also served, including fried chicken, bulgogi, rice balls, and much more… I definitely felt like I was in Korea instead of Hong Kong!

There were also loads of fun Korean mini-games adapted from reality TV shows for student participants to try.

“There are indeed many people out there who are familiar with Korean culture and absolutely love it. But there are still those who are foreign to it and we would like them to experience what our culture is like.” When asked why she wanted to organise the first Korean Night at CityU, Sunhee Shin, the organising team-lead said she had always wanted to host a Korean cultural event, considering that the Korean student population is growing at CityU.

Apparently, the various cultural nights are not just for introducing new cultures, but are also representations of particular identities.

Of course, being a pioneer is never easy, and around every corner is a new challenge. This year’s Korean Night is just the beginning. Our Korean community is excited to bring more joy to the students of CityU at future events!

Taiwanese Night – 28 March 2018

“Hurry up, there’s already a long queue!”

I remember sending this message to my friends after seeing how many people were already outside the venue – even before Taiwanese Night had officially begun. After all, who doesn’t love bubble milk tea and Taiwanese snacks?

Taiwanese Night has always been an eagerly awaited event at CityU. Whether it’s because of the cuisine or the pop music, it is widely enjoyed by students of different backgrounds. No one could hide their enjoyment when the Taiwanese students went around the venue distributing street snacks. However, the excitement and enthusiasm of the crowd really increased when a classic Taiwanese song came on during the performance. It truly brought back nostalgic childhood memories for many of the students from Taiwan.

“We want the event to roll out like a show, where everyone can enjoy the performances we put together for the night.” Lee Yen Ting, Tim, the main organiser said the Taiwanese Night was useful in making Taiwanese culture more prominent to the campus community.

Whether it’s Japanese Day, Korean Night, Taiwanese Night, or any of the other amazing SIP events held on campus, every organising team shares the same vision – to advocate and support student integration at CityU. No matter who you are and where you are from, there must be something new to learn about the world and yourself through understanding and interacting with people from different cultures.

To all students at CityU, we encourage you to apply for SIP so that you can host many more awesome events next year!
