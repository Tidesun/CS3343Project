Off Campus Peer Support Ambassadors:
Building “Home” away from Home

Imagine, stepping into a whole new city, being away from your family and friends and moving into a neighbourhood away from campus that you do not know much about. You probably have a lot of questions spinning in your head and you wonder where to start getting to know the people and this whole new environment surrounding you. This is where Off-campus Peer Support Ambassadors (OPSA) programme comes in. The programme is part of the university’s effort to promote integration and cultural cohesion among students. Through OPSA, students from various backgrounds living off-campus have one more platform to connect, socialize with each other and learn about Hong Kong and its culture.

How to build a community out of the campus blocks?

Through a variety of events and activities, the goal of the programme is to help students living off-campus build a supportive, diverse and international community.

Student hosts in the programme take the lead on hosting events and activities regularly for OPSA participants so that they can easily get into the social circle and have more opportunities to explore and learn about Hong Kong with their peers. Student hosts come up with a variety of activities such as hiking, football practices, cycling, sightseeing, hotpot dinner gatherings and voluntary services. Through social media and website, student hosts also publish interesting and informative stories containing tips such as “how to get a sim card” or “where you can get the best food in town” among the OPSA community.


OPSA student hosts in groups of charity, entertainment and food

A Peer Support Network for Non-local Students

Non-local undergraduate and exchange students of CityU who live off campus are very welcome to join the programme while the OPSA student host team is made up of senior year students who are familiar with CityU and Hong Kong. For non-local students who are new to the city, apart from the information provided by the university and googled from the internet, a support network consists of peers is no doubt an invaluable resource. OPSA hosts are just like off-campus mentors to whom they can ask questions like how to apply for bank cards and how to settle water bill payments.

In this peer support network, non-local students could find peers who share similar experiences. By celebrating together in times of joy and helping out each other at moments of challenges, participants and student hosts will gain takeaways from the programme, be it making new friends, learning more about different cultures and understanding local life


OPSA student hosts in groups of sightseeing and sports

Building “Home” away from Home

In today’s globalized world, traveling, study abroad and migration to a new country have become very common and many of us would have the opportunity to experience living in a different city or country. With advancement of technology and transportation, we are more connected than ever and there are more tools to help us get to know a new place quickly and stay in touch with our family and friends without worries about geographical distance. However, for many of us, no matter where we go, sense of belonging is still very important and it’s always nice to have a “home” to go back to. There are lots of interpretation about the concept of “home”, but most of us would agree that home should be about security, support, warmth and love. In OPSA, although there isn’t a physical space that connects all, there is the care that connects the students, not confined by differences among individuals.
