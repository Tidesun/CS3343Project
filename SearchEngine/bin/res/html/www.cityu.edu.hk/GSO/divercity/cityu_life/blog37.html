The Insider’s Guide to Exchange Study @ CityU

Text and photos: Silvia Szu-ya Huang,
exchange student from National Chengchi University, Taiwan

My exchange study at CityU in Hong Kong has been a significant time in my life. The lectures at CityU, making friends with people from different parts of the world, exploring Hong Kong and traveling abroad have all made a difference in my life. How?

The first thing to know: one semester is short and the coursework can be very intensive. However, there are still HOLIDAYS! Although Hong Kong has always been a Chinese society, unlike Taiwan, it was a British colony before 1997, so both Eastern and Western holidays are celebrated here. Therefore, you should definitely learn how to manage your time and prioritize tasks so that you can both study and explore the city during your few months in Hong Kong.

I would like to share some interesting parts of my exchange experience in Hong Kong. Here is my list of recommendations.

Study

Although many of my exchange fellows consider exchange as a period for them to travel and explore the world (everyone has their own expectations), study has been an important part of my exchange. At CityU, the minimum study requirement is 12 course credits per semester.

CityU only has one library, but there are a lot of open or semi-open study spaces and meeting rooms. Most resources are available to students, and you only need your student card to access them.

There are also printers in the library and the computer center. Each exchange student is granted 1,500 pages for printing on campus, and the printing facilities are easily accessible, which I found quite impressive!

  
(Left) Study areas in Li Dak Sum Yip Yio Chin Academic Building (LI)
(Right) You can print files using the public computers on campus with your student account and student ID card

Campus Life

There are a lot of activities for exchange students (or any students) at CityU. Most of the activities are offered on a first-come-first-served basis, and some have an entrance fee. I went on a daytrip to Lantau Island for non-local and international students and to Chinese Lunar New Year’s Night and different “nights” organized by different student populations on campus to celebrate and showcase different countries’ unique cultures (they offered free entry!). At the student residence, every hall has a student committee that organizes activities such as singing contests, picnics and high table dinners for its residents. You can register as a helper to help make these events happen, which is a great way to meet interesting people and practice your Cantonese!

Outside CityU

Off campus, there are a lot of things happening every day in Hong Kong. Below are some of the things I did. As an exchange student, you should use your time to explore Hong Kong and find what suits you best!

  • Take advantage of public events and learn outside the classroom

    I attended the General Assembly’s International Women’s Day Lightning Talks in Hong Kong. As a business student living in Hong Kong, one of the most important finance centers in the Asia Pacific region, I was inspired by the female speakers.

  • Exercise

    Apart from going to the gym at CityU, I also joined City Hunter, a group facilitated by the CityU Student Residence Office, which offers different exercise-related activities. Jogging is one of my favorite sports, and I often took the MTR to Taiwai and jogged along the riverbank all the way to the Chinese University of Hong Kong. I highly recommend this route if you love to jog. The view along the riverbank is very nice and relaxing.

  • Evening fun

    As a city that never sleeps, Hong Kong’s nightlife is as vivid as the city in the daytime, and you can always find something fun to do at night. In Mongkok, you can get a picture of a typical Hong Kong busy nighttime street from the street photography service providers there. If you are a foodie like me, do not miss the late night desserts – trust me, you have to try something with mango! If you come from a city where horseracing is not a common event, you can check out a horserace in person. The one I went to was full of excitement!

There is so much to share about what to do during exchange study at CityU and in Hong Kong. My exchange study in Hong Kong has been a great experience, and I have felt free and unfettered. However, you will need to know what you want to achieve from the exchange study programme so that you don’t get lost. I hope you have the best exchange experience!


There are so many things to do in Hong Kong – it’s up to you to decide how to make the most of your exchange study programme
