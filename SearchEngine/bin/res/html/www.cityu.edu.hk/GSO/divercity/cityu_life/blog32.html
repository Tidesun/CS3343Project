15 November 2017

Grease - Where Music and Dance Brought People Together

Text: Natasya Viona Chandra,
Quantitative Finance & Risk Management

Photos:
Andy Yang,
Business Economics

Student Development Services, City University of Hong Kong

Aufar Alif Waldi,

“Hurry up! There are so many people here already!” That was the last text sent by my friend before I rushed out of my room towards the Wong Cheung Lo Hui Yet Hall, where the musical Grease would be held this year. As part of the CityU Arts Festival 2017, this musical was the flagship performance with a talented cast consisting of thirty five students.

There were still 40 minutes before the gates were open, but the throng of people that were waiting was unbelievable. I heard the excited chatter and eager faces of people wanting to see their friends on the show. I was one of them. Last year, Fame was a huge success. Grease is such a classic and knowing that my friends had practised so hard for this day, I couldn’t wait to see what they had to offer.

This year, CityU brought along professionals from dance coordinators to choreographers and acting supervisors. Not only there was a full production team, there was a very diverse cast this year! From Indonesians, Koreans, Indians and locals, everyone worked together for this long-awaited Broadway musical. The 1950s settings and costumes brought the whole atmosphere together, and as my friends and I waited for the show to start, the hall began filling slowly, and by the time the lights were off, it was a full house!


The audience were not let down by this year’s performance, for sure!

I had the privilege to go backstage and talk to some of the casts before and after the show. Here’s what they had to say about the show!

This year, we have a mix of internationals and locals working together to present Grease. How do you feel working with such a diverse cast?

It’s different but exciting! Everyone brings something to the table. Not everyone is a musical veteran, some of us joined because we wanted to experience something new, and boy, we made it! We had an orientation camp in Macau during which we talked about things that are outside the musical and everyone bonded over that. It’s great working with people of different nationalities. We get to share diverse cultural backgrounds, and we believe we grow a little bit more when we meet new people!


A sneak-peek into their practice session!

For you first-timers in musicals, how do you feel being a part of the show?

We heard a good response from last year’s cast of the musical Fame, so we wanted to try out this year! There is definitely pressure because some of our casts also starred in the previous year musical, but everyone filled each other’s gaps. People who are good in dancing teach people who struggle with it, and at the end of the day, we go back with a smile on our face because we know we’ve worked hard.


Gotta make sure the vocals are on point!

What was the biggest challenge in the production?

It’s so hard to sing and dance at the same time! Moreover, we need to make sure we’re in sync and in harmony. We wanted to excel the singing part so badly we would sing on the streets and even while walking to the MTR! However, we’ve been in training since summer, and that’s when we built our foundation. It’s one month till the performance, and everyone has improved so much! Everyone also devoted a lot of their time to this production.

Would you recommend other friends to join the CityU musical next year?

YES! If you’re interested in performing, learning new skills in theatre arts and making lots of friends, this is the platform for you to do so. Don’t be shy and just sign up for it! Even if you are shy, it is an excellent way to get out of your shell. Don’t be afraid to mess up too! If you do one thing right, you get the confidence to do everything else right. Treasure your chance and shine on stage.


Treasure your chance and shine on stage!

Lastly, can you describe your whole Grease experience in short?

AMAZING! We have faith that it will be a good show because we are trained by professionals, worked hard and the chemistry between the casts is just amazing!
