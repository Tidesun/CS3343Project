Savour the “Good Old Days” in Nam Shan Estate

Text and photos: Joyce Shuk-yin Ho,
English Studies

Edit: Richard Finnigan,
Media & Communication

Feeling bored of tedious strolls through Festival Walk and mindlessly eating at the food court, with those familiar chain restaurants your only option? It sounds like a time for an adventure, let’s have a little getaway to Nam Shan Estate, where you can find a quintessential smattering of small local businesses offering affordable, delectable food options and services to the community.

Nam Shan Estate: Beyond A Public Housing District

Nam Shan Estate, a long-established public housing estate with 40 years of history, is located between Kowloon Tong and Shek Kip Mei. It has recently gained popularity on social media for its sought-after photographing spots, including a spacious vacant playground with old-fashioned playground/park facilities for children.

Most notably, Nam Shan Estate provides a wide range of affordable and delicious food options. Consequently, it’s rather common to notice CityU students hustling to the neighbourhood for a quick bite or a dinner get-together. It’s no surprise then, that Nam Shan has been nicknamed as the “the backyard of CityU”.

By now you’re likely wondering how to get to this scenic little area!

Follow these steps from CityU to reach Nam Shan Estate:

  1. On 4th floor of Yeung Kin Man Academic Building (YEUNG Building), find Lift 16/17.
  2. Turn right to the alley and walk until the very end.
  3. Turn right and push open the door, you will see a lift.
  4. Take the lift to 1/F.
  5. Turn left when you get out of the lift and you will leave CityU campus.
  6. Go across the street, turn left and walk until you see the stairs.
  7. Walk down the stairs, turn left and go straight ahead.
  8. You will then see Nam Lok House, which is the entrance of Nam Shan Estate.

Upon arriving, you may feel as if you’ve stumbled into a time capsule and returned to the 1970’s: the rectangular blocks of flats with green coloured facades, typical old-fashioned Hong Kong bakeries, restaurants and grocery stores selling snacks that many would remember from their childhood. All these features give this community a unique character and a real feel of peacefulness and an escape from the noise and fast pace of the society. This special kind of atmosphere can be found in such neighbourhoods formed within old public housing estates, remembered fondly by many local Hong Kong people.

Apart from its architectural style, what makes Nam Shan Estate especially attractive is the local food options it presents. Though they may not be of gourmet quality, if you are a local to Hong Kong, familiar with the grassroot lifestyle in a public housing estate, they will take you down memory lane and let you relive those times past you once shared.

An Exploration Guide for CityU Students

To help those of you who are interested in knowing more about Nam Shan Estate and exploring the neighbourhood, here is a list of options I have compiled for you to explore the neighbourhood.

Part I Food

“Grab a Quick Bite” Options:

On Lok Bakery (安樂麵飽餅店)

In this nostalgic bakery, they sell a variety of mouth-watering baked goods, including crusty egg tarts, buttery cookies, flaky pineapple buns, fulfilling scones and their famously addictive Swiss rolls. The owners are friendly and will happily introduce the delicious goodies if you are overwhelmed by a sea of choices.

Address: Shop 110, G/F, Nam Lok House, Nam Shan Estate
Opening Hours: 6:30am - 8:00pm

C+ HOUSE Ice Cream (C+屋)

A taste of some delectable icy treats will definitely keep you cool under Hong Kong’s blazing sun! With its down-to-earth pricing and extensive ice cream selections, this store has gained a solid reputation for their considerate services and the authentic ice cream flavours.

Address: Shop P20, Podium, Nam Shan Shopping Centre, Nam Shan Estate
Opening Hours: 12:00nn - 7:00pm (Rest on Sundays)

“Dine with a Group of Friends” Choices:

Ban Heung Lau Restaurant (品香樓中西風味餐廳)

Ban Heung Lau is a well-known restaurant among CityU students, as it mainly offers set lunches around noon and modern Chinese dishes at night. Ban Heung Lau is often packed with a diverse range of clientele, longing for the flavourful stir-fry dishes and the crispy deep-fried pork ribs.

Address: Shop 204-205, 210-213, Nam Fung House, Nam Shan Estate
Tel: 2778 8791
Opening Hours: 6:45am - 12:00mn

Nam Fung Restaurant (南豐餐廳)

For a taste of reminiscing interior design, stroll into a quintessentially homey Cha Chaan Teng, a rather affordable spin for Ban Heung Lau. The enormous portions coupled with promotions of offering 2 bowls of rice per dish, own the hearts of the nearby residents.

Address: Shop 208-209, Nam Fung House, Nam Shan Estate
Tel: 2778 7196
Opening Hours: 7:00am - 11:00pm

Chillazy Dessert (糖室)

With shimmering fairy lights hanging over the outer space and a classic, wooden themed environment, Chillazy Dessert offers a relaxing environment to eat the cooling shaved ice, green tea soufflé and more delicate desserts. This dessert restaurant presents a different vibe from the Dai Pai Dongs (open-air food stalls) outside.

Address: Shop C4, Cooked Food Stall, Podium, Nam Shan Shopping Centre, Nam Shan Estate
Tel: 5260 8053
Opening Hours: 3:00pm - 12:00mn

Late Night Snacks:

Due to the Government’s strict regulation on street vendors, there are fewer and fewer street food hawkers in Hong Kong. For decades, hawking has served as a means for the lower-income class to make a living and it embodies the spirit of determination in times of hardship among Hong Kong people. The in-expensive, ready-to-eat snacks and drinks at food stalls are often considered as one of the most important part in the local food culture.

From around 9 pm until midnight, street food hawkers will gather in Nam Shan Estate Shopping Center Podium selling authentic Hong Kong snacks. Here is the list of goodies you can find in our very own heaven of late night eating:

Siu Mai and Steamed Buns

Siu Mai is one of the must-eat traditional Chinese dim sums. Many food stalls selling Siu Mai use pre-made ones made of minced fish but this lady at Nam Sham Estate sells handmade minced beef Siu Mai. You can even see her making Siu Mai in front of you!

Chinese Dessert

This stall sells freshly made Chinese-style desserts such as sago soup, a mixture of sago, coconut milk and evaporated milk, you may also order it with mangos and silky tofu pudding.

Egg Waffles

Egg waffle is definitely a signature Hong Kong street delight. With HK$ 8, you can get a nice portion of waffles that are enough to fill your stomach. If you are a sponge cake lover, you will love the egg waffles!

Part II Daily Necessities

There are a few local stores at Nam Shan Estate where you can buy daily necessities. These stores are commonly found in old residential estates, which typically cater for local needs and sell reasonably priced products to the residents living nearby.

Snacks, Drinks and Toys:

Nam Cheong Groceries (南昌辦館)

Tip: Have you ever tried powdered Coca-Cola candy before? They sell it.

Address: Shop 11, G/F, Nam Lok House, Nam Shan Estate
Opening Hours: 9:30am - 9:30pm (Closed on Sundays)

Hap Cheong Lung Groceries (合昌隆雜貨)

Tip: Looking to store some soft drinks, cup-noodles and crackers in hall or at home? This may be the place to go to.

Address: Shop 111, G/F, Nam Fung House, Nam Shan Estate
Opening Hours: 9:30am - 8:30pm

Toilet Paper Rolls, Kitchen and Cleaning Supplies, Chinese Medicine:

Lam Kan Kei Pharmacy (林勤記大藥行)

Address: Shop 111, G/F, Nam Lok House, Nam Shan Estate
Opening Hours: 8:30am - 8:30pm (Rest on Saturdays)

Family Appliances:

Kwok Cheong Home Accessories (國昌家品總匯)

Goods: Storage boxes, electronic fans, utensil sets.

Address: Shop 112, G/F, Nam Lok House, Nam Shan Estate
Opening Hours: 9:30am - 7:30pm

Lei Sing Hardware (利成五金)

Goods: Water buckets, kettles, WD-40 (penetrating oil and water-displacing spray).

Address: Shop 115, G/F, Nam Lok House, Nam Shan Estate
Opening Hours: 10:00am - 7:00pm

Stationeries, Styrofoam, Wrapping paper, Decorations:

Tai Yam Hong Stationary (大任行文具舖)

Address: Shop 202, Podium, Nam Lok House, Nam Shan Estate
Tel: 2777 5037
Opening Hours: 10:00am - 6:45pm

When the sun sets, Nam Shan Estate becomes more attractive through a touch of glamour from the household lights. The residents, businesses and even visitors such as CityU students can all share a collective memory that has been created, recreated and passed down through generations. At first glance, the outlook of the neighbourhood and its atmosphere may feel nostalgic, and to some of us, they bring the fond memories of the “good old days” of ours living in this city. It’s lucky that our University’s campus location is right at the intersection of several neighbourhoods as we can get easy access to many restaurants and businesses offering different services.

With more urban redevelopment projects initiated and implemented, more neighbourhoods formed in old public housing estates may disappear as time goes by. This is probably why as I am researching about CityU neighbourhoods, I feel that there is a strong necessity to write about Nam Shan Estate. It is refreshing to explore places around the campus, to experience (or even re-experience) the urban grassroots in our city living their lives peacefully in such hustle and bustle. The food and places I included on the list probably would not appear on any travel recommendation lists, but I guess a lot of us CityU students would feel lucky that this lovely neighbourhood is so close to us and we get to live and share a part of the history that has not yet been engulfed by the modern society.
