Kowloon: Through the Eyes of a Photographer

Text and photos: Andy Yang,
Business Administration

As a photographer, I always wanted to do a photo essay on some of my favourite spots in the Kowloon area of Hong Kong. Some of the spots are already well-known, but there are also some spots that have not been widely introduced. If you go at different times of the day, you will get very different shots because of the changing lights and activities.

This is a shot from the path to the top of Beacon Hill. Beacon Hill is located right behind CityU’s student dormitory. It takes around 1 hour to reach the top. Make sure to bring headlamps when you’re doing a night hike. - Stay safe and have fun!

Temple Street Night Market in Yau Ma Tei is famous for its popular street bazaar and some of the cheap food places it offers. You can also get some touristy products there. At night, together with some of the remaining neon lights, the lights of the market would not disappoint any photographer who wants to capture a moment of the vividness of the city.

    

Fa Yuen Street Market in Mongkok is a place where there are quite a number of local shops and it is an alternative to Temple Street if you are interested in photographing busy, crowded street scenes of Hong Kong. Apart from tourist-oriented shops there are also a number of stores and boutiques that offer products at low prices. I guess that’s one of the reasons why both local people and tourists enjoy going to Mongkok!

I have always enjoyed street photography and in Hong Kong, with all sorts of people from around the world, there is never a shortage of moments that I find worthy of capturing. Tsim Sha Tsui, for example, is one of the places I visit often with my camera. This is a woman enjoying the beautiful view of Central from the upper deck of the Tsim Sha Tsui Promenade.

The Tsim Sha Tsui Promenade is also a great place to enjoy sunset, although there is a great chance that you will be accompanied by a big crowd.

As a resident, I probably want to avoid busy, noisy districts but as a photographer, the crowded streets in Mongkok, Yau Ma Tei and Tsim Sha Tsui provide me with a lot of inspirations. I never know which passerby would be my next subject, or whether tomorrow’s weather condition would be good enough for a shot at a particular place.
