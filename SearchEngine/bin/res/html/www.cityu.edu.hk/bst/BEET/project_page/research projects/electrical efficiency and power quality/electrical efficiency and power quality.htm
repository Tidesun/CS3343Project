Electrical Efficiency and Power Quality

 

With the trend of using distributed generation system in modern gird and controlling the energy usage of the public, a new generation Advanced Metering Infrastructure (AMI) is needed. The basic motivation and idea behind the AMI is linking the consumer and the gird (utility companies) together for seamless interaction for enhancing the quality of services and saving energy generation and utilization cost. AMI also offers other functions like time-based pricing, consumption data for consumer and utility companies, power quality monitoring, remote control operation from utility companies, tamper and energy theft detection and the capability to communicate with other intelligent devices in the home.

 The AMI starts with smart meters to retrieving the correct information from the end-point of the gird and, after that the information collected will be transmitted back to the Meter Data Management System through the Communication Infrastructure. As a key element of the AMI, the smart meter determines the functionality and ability of the whole system. A prototype meter was developed for this purpose as shown in Fig. 1.

Figure 1: Novel Smart Meter Prototype

 This smart meter is capable to provide most of the functionality same as the High-End PQ Meter available in the market nowadays, including power data, harmonics, sag, swell, notches, transients and compressed waveforms. This meter is capable to provide real-time electrical data monitoring to the operator miles away through various communication means. The architecture overview of the meter is shown in Fig. 2.

Figure 2: Architecture overview of the novel smart meter

The meter uses an efficient Codec for data compression which reduces data rate for use with low-bit rate communication network like Zigbee. In the laboratory test, electrical waveforms captured by the smart meter are transmitted back to the server through Zigbee network. One example is shown in Fig. 3.
