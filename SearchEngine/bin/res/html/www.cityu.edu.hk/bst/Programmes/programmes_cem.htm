Associate of Science in Construction Engineering and Management (ASc CEM)

Mode of Study: Full-time
Normal duration: 2 years
Programme Leader: Dr Andy Ng
Deputy Programme Leader: Mr Gary Chan
Tuition fee: Click here for more information
General enquiry: bsinfo@cityu.edu.hk (e-mail) / 3442 7089 (hotline)



Programme Aims

The principal aim of the programme is to provide a sound education and vocational training for students to become technically competent personnel in the construction industry. It equips students with engineering knowledge, design process, construction technology, quantity surveying and management skills required for careers in building and other forms of construction, and provides a broad-based academic foundation for further academic and career development.



Career Prospects

Graduates of this programme will have the necessary technical and academic foundation to develop successful careers, in the private and public sectors, in a wide range of construction and related fields, including project management, construction engineering, contract administration, quantity surveying, project co-ordination and field supervision.



Admission Requirements

To be eligible for admission, you must satisfy the Entrance Requirements.

Programme Structure & Curriculum

Main Subject Areas

  • Construction Technology
  • Construction Management
  • Structural Mechanics and Design
  • Geotechnical and Foundation Engineering
  • Temporary Works Design
  • Computer Programming
  • Mathematics and Operational Research
  • Environmental Science and Building Services
  • Law, Economics and Management
  • Integrated Project
  • English Language
  • Chinese Language and Chinese Civilisation
  • Construction Communication
  • Site Suveying
  • Industrial Training

In addition to the University Language Requirements and Gateway Education Requirements, supporting areas include appropriate studies in Construction Communication, Site Surveying and Industrial Training.

Professional Accreditation and Recognition

This programme has been successfully accredited by the Hong Kong Institution of Engineers (HKIE) and the Hong Kong Institute of Construction Managers (HKICM). Graduates of the programme are accepted as meeting the academic requirements for the Associate Membership of these two professional bodies.

This programme has also been recognized by the Chartered Institute of Building (CIOB). Students and graduates of the programme are accepted as student members of the CIOB. Graduates achieved a CIOB accredited bachelor degree or equivalent programme will then be able to progress to full membership.

Articulation

This programme is also recognized by local universities and many overseas institutions for admission with advanced standing up to third year of their degree programmes. The admission of a candidate is subject to the selection criteria of individual institution and the candidate's own merits.
