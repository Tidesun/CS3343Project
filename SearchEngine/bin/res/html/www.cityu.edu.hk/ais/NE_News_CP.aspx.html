Seminars & Expert Talks

Year:
AIS PhD student, Mr Christoph Andreas NEUSIEDL will give a talk "Education for the 'Pluriverse': Unschooling as anarchistic practice" at Goldsmiths, University of London on 29 October 2018.
(29 Oct 2018, Monday, Goldsmiths, University of London)
Dr Yuk Wah CHAN was invited to be guest speaker at Christopher K. Ho’s installation presentation, CX 888, at de Sarthe Gallery, 23 August 2018. The event was designed to meet with the artist and audience for incubating ideas on Chinese diasporas, reverse migration, and the intersection of nationalism and identity.
(23 Aug 2018, Thursday, de Sarthe Gallery)
Prof Mark R THOMPSON, AIS head/SEARC director gave a talk on 5 July 2018 entitled 'Democracy's Discontents in Southeast Asia' at the Institute of Security and International Studies, Chulalongkorn University.
(5 Jul 2018, Thursday, Institute of Security and International Studies, Chulalongkorn University)
Prof Mark R THOMPSON, AIS head/SEARC director gave a talk 'Switching sides: The Philippines' pivot to China under Duterte' at the Asian Governance Foundation, Faculty of Law, Thammasat University 4 July 2018.
(4 Jul 2018, Wednesday, Asian Governance Foundation, Faculty of Law, Thammasat University)
Prof Mark R THOMPSON, AIS Head/SEARC Director gave a lecture on 14 June 2018 entitled "Duterte's Violent Populism: Legitimacy and Injustice in the Philippines" as part of the 2018 Philippine Studies Summer Lectures series, Department of Southeast Asian Studies, Humboldt Universitaet zu Berlin.
(14 Jun 2018, Thursday, Humboldt Universitaet zu Berlin)
Prof Mark R THOMPSON, AIS Head/SEARC Director gave a lecture on 11 June 2018 entitled "Democracy's Discontents in Southeast Asia" as part of the "Politics and Development in Southeast Asia" lecture series at the University of Rostock, Germany.
(11 Jun 2018, Monday, University of Rostock, Germany)
Prof Mark R THOMPSON, AIS Head/SEARC Director gave a lecture on 4 June 2018 entitled "Duterte's Pivot to China: Towards a New Regional Order" to the China Centrum Tuebingen and took part in a roundtable discussion at the Centrum the following day on the looming China-U.S. trade war.

(4 Jun 2018, Monday, China Centrum Tuebingen (CCT))
Dr Yuk Wah CHAN presented a seminar entitled “Sweetness, Maids and Power: Globalization of First World Kitchen and Care Work” at the Department of Anthropology, The Chinese University of Hong Kong on 6 April 2018.
(6 Apr 2018, Friday, Department of Anthropology, The Chinese University of Hong Kong )
Prof Mark R THOMPSON, AIS Head and SEARC Director, was invited as guest speaker to discuss and share thoughts about the political developments in Southeast Asia at Credit Suisse 21st Asian Investment Conference (AIC) on 21 March 2018.
(21 Mar 2018, Wednesday, Credit Suisse Asian Investment Conference)
Dr Kobe CHAN was invited to give guest lectures on Japanese Cinema at The University of Hong Kong on 15 November and 22 November 2017.
(15 Nov 2017, Wednesday, The University of Hong Kong)
Prof Mark R THOMPSON, AIS head/SEARC director gave a lunch talk at the Foreign Correspondents' Club, Hong Kong on Tuesday, 7 November 2017 at 12:30 pm about the Duterte administration in the Philippines.
(7 Nov 2017, Tuesday, The Foreign Correspondents’ Club, Hong Kong)

Lunch talk details: https://www.fcchk.org/event/club-lunch-dutertes-violent-populism-in-the-philippines/

Mark R Thompson, AIS head/SEARC director was an invited participant for the “Roundtable: Southeast Asian Elections, Populism and
Authoritarianism" at the 9th Europeans Southeast Asian (EuroSEAS) Conference, Oxford University, 16-18 August 2017. At the same conference he also presented papers on "Duterte's 'Right' Populism in the Philippines" and on "The Anti-Marcos Struggle Revisited".
(16 Aug 2017, Wednesday, EuroSEAS Conference 2017 at The University of Oxford )
Prof Mark R THOMPSON, AIS head/SEARC director was invited to give a Berkeley Southeast Asia Program Southeast Asia Program talk "Duterte's violent 'right' populism in the Philippines" on 4 April 2017.
(4 Apr 2017, Tuesday, University of California, Berkeley)
Prof Mark R THOMPSON, AIS head/SEARC director was invited to give a Southeast Asia Program (SEAP) Gatty Lecture "Democracy and its Discontents in Southeast Asia" at the Cornell University on 23 March 2017.

(23 Mar 2017, Thursday, Cornell University)
Dr CHAN Yuk Wah was invited as visiting scholar at SAPMiC (Sydney Asia Pacific Migration Centre, The University of Sydney), 14-24 March 2017, to provide her expertise on migration dynamics and patterns in Asia, and exchange with migration scholars at different events.
(14 Mar 2017, Tuesday, University of Sydney)

(12 Mar 2017, Sunday, ifva Film Festival)
Prof Mark R THOMPSON, Head of AIS/SEARC Director was invited to give a talk on Philippine politics at the prestigious Weatherhead East Asian Institute, Columbia University on Wednesday 15 February 2017.
(15 Feb 2017, Wednesday, Columbia University)
Prof Mark R Thompson, Head of AIS/SEARC Director published an article on the suspension of the "war of drugs" in the Philippines in The Conversation

(14 Feb 2017, Tuesday, The Conversation)
AIS co-hosted a presentation by Parag Khanna , Author of Connectography: Mapping the Future of Global Civilization & Senior Research Fellow, Centre on Asia and Globalisation, Lee Kuan Yew School of Public Policy, National University of Singapore at the Hong Kong Asia Society on June 13, 2016. The event was attended by about 60 people including several AIS students and AIS organizer Dr Justin Robertson, Prof Graeme Lang (former head of AIS), Prof William Case (former acting head of AIS) and AIS Head Prof Mark R Thompson who gave the closing remarks at the event.
(13 Jun 2016, Monday, Hong Kong Asia Society )
Poetry Reading by Dr Birgit Linder at Dymocks Books and Cafe in Discovery Bay on 30 January 2016

(13 Oct 2015, Tuesday, Balsillie School of International Affairs)
Dr Ruben GONZALEZ VICENTE was invited to participate the workshop of the Labour, Capital and South-South Development: Emergent Polycentrism in the Global Economy at the Cornell University from 9-10 October 2015.
(9 Oct 2015, Friday, Cornell University)

(8 Oct 2015, Thursday, Binghamton University)

(24 Sep 2015, Thursday, Cambridge University)

(19 Sep 2015, Saturday, Society of Japanese Language Education Hong Kong)
Dr Thomas PATTON will give a lecture on 'Monks, Meditation and Magic in Contemporary Burmese Buddhism' on 15 August 2015 at Karin Weber Gallery.
(15 Aug 2015, Saturday, Karin Weber Gallery)

Dr Thomas PATTON gave a talk on 'Buddhist Perceptions of the Body' on 7 May 2015 at the Hong Kong Insight Meditation Centre.
(7 May 2015, Thursday, Hong Kong Insight Meditation Centre)
