Sunday, 21  Oct  2018

 

The Career and Leadership Centre offers one-to-one career consultation services to students by appointment. Students are welcome to discuss career and further study concerns with our professional Career Advisors who are experienced in guiding graduate recruitment in the current job market. You may seek their advice regarding:

 

 

  •          Resume Writing
  •         Job Application Letters
  •          Job Interview Skills
  •          Mock Interview Practice
  •          Assessment Centre
  •         Psychometric Tests
        (Aptitude tests such as verbal, numerical, critical reasoning tests)
  •         Personality Tests
  •        Further study Advising
  •          Academic Exchange
  •         Internship & Placement
  •        Job Search Skills

 

How to make Appointments


Please make your appointment by phone @ 34425591 during Mon-Fri office hours (09:00  - 17:30, except Saturday, Sunday and public holidays).

 

Remarks:


  • The service is eligible for non-CB students.  CB students may seek similar service from CB Career Service.
  • The appointment schedule will be released every Tuesday.  Please click here to check it out. 

 

Eligibility


·   Our career consultation services are open for current CityU undergraduate and postgraduate students (non-CB), and our alumni (including CB students) who have graduated not more than six months.



Preparation for the Consultation


·   Please prepare well your resume and cover letter (in hard copy) and bring them to the career consultation to maximize your learning. If you have an interview for a job, please bring along the job requirement.

 


Appointment Policy


·   While we encourage your participation, we do not encourage dependency. Full-time students may take not more than 4 consultation sessions in a semester. If students have special needs, they can apply at the service counter to seek special approval from the Career and Leadership Centre.  No more than 4 exemption requests for seeking additional consultation sessions will be accommodated per student in one academic year.  Fresh graduates may use our career consultation services within six months after their graduation and a maximum of 6 consultation sessions.

 

·   Part-time or combined mode undergraduate and postgraduate students can now take not more than 2 consultation sessions in a semester.  Please confine your appointment in the morning sessions (9:00 a.m. – 12:00 noon).      However, exemption requests cannot be accommodated and their eligibility for making consultation appointments will be expired after graduation.  



Cancellation / No Show Policy


·   To encourage student to be a responsible person and to release the booking to other students in need, students should cancel their booking in the Appointment system or by calling us at 3442 5591.


  • Cancellation policy: Cancellation after 12:00 noon with 1 working day prior to your scheduled appointment (excluding weekends and public holidays), you will not be eligible for making appointment in the following two weeks.

  • No-show student (who does not show up and without prior cancellation) will not be eligible for making consultation appointment in the following threeweeks.

  • Students who arrive more than 20 minutes late will not be eligible for making consultation appointment in the following two weeks.

***  For students who are not observing the above cancellation policy, their prior secured consultation sessions within the penalty period will be voided.

 

 

Honesty Policy


Please understand that we have put in extra care and efforts on offering one-on-one consultation sessions.  Do not attempt to get around our policy by faking your identity, number of consultation sessions conducted, your study mode, etc.  We have high expectation on our students’ honesty and integrity.  With any violation of this policy, students are subject to the University Student Code of Conduct.
